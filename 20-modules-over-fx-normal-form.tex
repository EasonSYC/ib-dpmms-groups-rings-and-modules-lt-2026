\section{Matrices over \(\FF\brac{x}\) and Normal Forms of Matrices}

In a previous example, we considered a vector space \(V\) over a field \(\FF\). Fix \(\alpha \in \End\para{V}\), we may construct a \(\FF\brac{x}\) module
\[
    \function{\cdot}{\FF\brac{x} \times V}{V}{\para{f, v}}{f\para{\alpha} \cdot v.}
\]

We call this module \(V_{\alpha}\).

If \(\dim V\) is finite, then \(V_{\alpha}\) is finitely generated as a \(\FF\brac{x}\) module.

\begin{example}
    Suppose \(V_{\alpha}\) is the module
    \[
        V_{\alpha} \isomorphic \FF\brac{x} / \para{x^r}.
    \]

    As a \(\FF\)-vector space, the right-hand side has basis
    \[
        1, x, x^2, \ldots, x^{r - 1}.
    \]  

    The endomorphism \(\functiondomain{\alpha}{V}{V}\) acts as multiplication by \(x\), i.e.,
    \[
        \function{\alpha}{\FF\brac{x} / \para{x^r}}{\FF\brac{x} / \para{x^r}}{f}{xf.}
    \]

    Take the endomorphism in the basis \(\brce{1, x, \ldots, x^{r - 1}}\) as a matrix
    \[
        \alpha = \begin{pmatrix}
            0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
            1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
            0 & 1 & \ddots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
            \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\
            0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
            0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 1 & 0
        \end{pmatrix}.
    \]
\end{example}

\begin{example}
    Suppose now instead
    \[
        V_{\alpha} \isomorphic \FF\brac{x} / \para{x - \lambda}^r.
    \]

    Now we choose \(\functiondomain{\beta}{V}{V}\) such that \(\beta = \alpha - \lambda \identity\). Then there is a basis for \(V\), where
    \[
        \beta = \begin{pmatrix}
            0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
            1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
            0 & 1 & \ddots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
            \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\
            0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 & 0 & 0 \\
            0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 1 & 0
        \end{pmatrix}.
    \]

    We note that in this basis, \(\alpha\) is a Jordan block of the form
    \[
        \alpha = \begin{pmatrix}
            \lambda & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
            1 & \lambda & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
            0 & 1 & \ddots & 0 & 0 & 0 \\
            \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\
            0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 & \lambda & 0 \\
            0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 1 & \lambda
        \end{pmatrix}.
    \]
\end{example}
